System

How the machine works on itself, and what the thinking costs.

Self-improvement

Recursive self-improvement (RSI), the literal kind: OpenThomas rewrites its own decision parameters. A change ships only after it beats the champion on leak-free replay of held-out days — and rolls back if live results disagree. Nothing below was chosen by a human.

Compute

What the thinking cost. Forecasting runs every cycle; replay is the expensive one — it re-forecasts historical days to score a candidate prompt before promoting it.

Paper mode. Fills are simulated at the real bid/ask, on live venue data, against a $100,000 book. No customer money is at risk, and none of this is advice.

Open all the way down. The harness, the risk engine, and every strategy parameter are MIT-licensed; the model it thinks with ships open weights; the models it trains on its own journal are released the same way. The source of every number on this page is one click away.
